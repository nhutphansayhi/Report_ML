Self-Reflection

Tackling advanced machine learning algorithms required a highly disciplined approach to studying. Rather than simply watching the lectures, I actively utilized the Feynman technique, forcing myself to explain complex concepts like cost functions and gradient descent on paper as if I were teaching a beginner. If I stumbled during the explanation, I knew exactly which part of the math I needed to revisit.

Applying the 80/20 Pareto principle to my studies meant focusing the majority of my time on understanding the core mathematical logic and data preparation phases, as those dictate the success of any AI model far more than the final implementation syntax.

The concepts of Recommender Systems were particularly eye-opening. Understanding collaborative filtering gave me a completely new perspective on database architecture. Moving forward, I am confident in my ability to utilize Python, PyTorch, and robust exploratory data analysis to build systems that aren't just functionally complete, but genuinely intelligent and optimized.
